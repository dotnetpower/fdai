HIL card - severity affordance variants

Top and left edge accents are not supported on content containers. Below are four compliant alternatives that keep severity local to the content through text, icons, pills, a complete border, or a whole-surface tint.

Same content in every card: critical T1 remediation resilience

A. Small severity glyph next to the title

A tiny colored square with an icon (! for critical, ▲ for high, ● for medium) sits just before the title. Scannable at a glance, and works if the pill is off-screen on narrow layouts.

D. Eyebrow line above the title

A one-line "CRITICAL · REMEDIATION" eyebrow sits above the card title in the severity color. Because that eyebrow already carries severity and category, the two right-side pills for the same values are hidden - fewer chips, more prose. This variant trades one visual channel (color) for another (typography).

Pick guide

  • C - fewest pixels, minimum-noise. Best if you trust the pill to always be visible (no truncation on narrow layouts).
  • B - adds a small icon so you can scan severity from the far left even if the row wraps. Good compromise between A and C.
  • D - keeps a color signal but moves it off the "list item bar" pattern. Nice for editorial / documentation feel.
  • E - ambient severity. Best when the queue is long and you want to feel "how hot is this page" at a glance.
  • F - most verbose, most self-explanatory - trades chips for words. Great for the HIL page where an operator lands cold.
